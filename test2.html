Chimera of the Post-Anthropocene

Marcie Begleiter

As a photographer and filmmaker my work has focused, in the past 3 years, on engaging with stressed ecosystems. Chimera of the Post-Anthropocene, my current series, explores objects that are assembled from nature but not of it. The photographic tableaus and short videos are based on specific biomes, envisioning dramatic and unpredictable futures of flora and fungi.


These hybrid sculptures are photographed in a style inspired by classic natural history museum dioramas. Recent work has been created in the fire scorched High Sierras of Yosemite, the sub-Alpine forests of Banff, Estero Bay in Central California and during a residency at the Sitka Center for Art and Ecology in Cascade Head, the estuary in Oregon formed by the confluence of the Salmon River and the Pacific.
